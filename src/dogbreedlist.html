  • Irish Wolfhound:


    Physical Aspects:
    Irish Wolfhounds are one of the tallest dog breeds, known for their impressive height and muscular build. They have a wiry coat that comes in various colors.
    Behavioral Traits:
    Despite their imposing size, Irish Wolfhounds are gentle giants. They are known for their calm and friendly demeanor, making them excellent companions. They are also quite dignified and reserved.

  • Irish Setter:


    Physical Aspects:
    Irish Setters are medium to large-sized dogs with a distinctive mahogany or chestnut-colored coat that is silky and feathery. They have an elegant and athletic build.
    Behavioral Traits:
    Irish Setters are energetic and playful, known for their friendly and outgoing nature. They are intelligent and require mental stimulation, making them responsive to training. They are also affectionate and get along well with families.

  • Irish Terrier:


    Physical Aspects:
    Irish Terriers are medium-sized dogs with a wiry, red coat. They have a compact and muscular build, giving them a robust appearance.
    Behavioral Traits:
    Irish Terriers are known for their spirited and confident personalities. They are loyal and make excellent watchdogs. Their intelligence and adaptability make them suitable for various activities, and they thrive on human companionship.

  • Soft-Coated Wheaten Terrier:


    Physical Aspects:
    Soft-Coated Wheaten Terriers are medium-sized dogs with a soft, silky, and wheaten-colored coat. They have a sturdy and square build.
    Behavioral Traits:
    These terriers are known for their friendly and outgoing nature. They are playful, yet trainable, making them great family pets. Soft-Coated Wheaten Terriers are social and enjoy being part of family activities.

  • Kerry Blue Terrier:


    Physical Aspects:
    Kerry Blue Terriers have a distinctive non-shedding, curly blue-gray coat. They have a compact and muscular build, with a characteristic beard and mustache.
    Behavioral Traits:
    Kerry Blue Terriers are known for their intelligence and spirited personality. They are loyal and protective, making them good watchdogs. They require early socialization and training to channel their energy positively.

  • Irish Water Spaniel:


    Physical Aspects:
    The Irish Water Spaniel has a curly, water-resistant coat that is usually liver-colored. They have a well-balanced and athletic build, with a distinctive curly topknot.
    Behavioral Traits:
    These spaniels are known for their love of water and strong swimming abilities. They are intelligent, curious, and trainable. Irish Water Spaniels make affectionate family pets and excel in water-related activities.

  • Glen of Imaal Terrier:


    Physical Aspects:
    Glen of Imaal Terriers are small to medium-sized dogs with a distinctive wheaten or blue coat. They have a sturdy build and a unique appearance.
    Behavioral Traits:
    Glen of Imaal Terriers are known for their bold and spirited nature. They are good with children and make loyal family companions. Their intelligence and agility make them suitable for various activities.

  • Irish Red and White Setter:


    Physical Aspects:
    Irish Red and White Setters have a distinctive coat with patches of deep red and white. They have a strong, athletic build with a graceful and elegant demeanor.
    Behavioral Traits:
    These setters are known for their friendly and outgoing personalities. They are intelligent, trainable, and thrive on human interaction. They are good with families and are particularly well-suited for outdoor activities.

  • Irish Glen of Imaal Terrier:


    Physical Aspects:
    Similar to the Glen of Imaal Terrier, this breed is known for its small to medium size and distinctive wheaten or blue coat. Behavioral Traits: Irish Glen of Imaal Terriers share the bold and spirited nature of their counterpart. They are affectionate, good with children, and make excellent companions. Their compact size makes them well-suited for both indoor and outdoor living.
